# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are tired parents or carers at bedtime, often using a phone or tablet in a dim bedroom while reading aloud to a child. The child is the experiencer, ages 1-10, but not the account holder, data subject for personalization, or independent navigator. The parent’s job is to choose a calm, age-appropriate illustrated story quickly and read it at their own pace with no ads, autoplay, or overstimulating interaction.

## Product Purpose

Mini Dream Time is a curated, read-together bedtime-story library with original recurring characters. It exists to preserve the shared bedtime reading ritual in a digital format: warm illustrated pages, parent-paced text, and stories that softly resolve toward sleep. Success means households return to favorite stories, repeat-read individual pages, trust the catalog, and pay for new curated content.

Primary launch domain: `minidreamtime.com`.

## Brand Personality

Warm, gentle, trustworthy. The voice is calm and parent-respecting, with quiet confidence rather than hype. It should feel like a premium picture book held under a warm bedside lamp: lovable, safe, softly magical, and never manipulative.

## Anti-references

Do not look like a developer tool, neon mission-control dashboard, generic AI generator, video app, game, streaming service, bright engagement-optimized kid app, ad-supported content farm, or franchise imitation. Avoid blue-white glare, hyperactive animation, reward loops, speech bubbles or baked-in text inside illustrations, generic prompt-pack aesthetics, and any visual resemblance to protected children’s properties or studio styles.

## Design Principles

1. Parent-paced calm over engagement. The product helps the night wind down; it never tries to extend the session.
2. App-layer text over clean art. Illustrations stay text-free so reading, localization, accessibility, and layout remain controlled by the product.
3. Original characters are the asset. Familiarity comes from recurring wholly owned characters, not borrowed likeness or brand-adjacent cues.
4. Privacy is part of the promise. No child data, no child profiling, no third-party ad SDKs, and no runtime personalization at launch.
5. Consistency beats novelty. Every story must feel like returning to the same small world, with characters staying on-model across scenes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target at least WCAG 2.2 AA for the parent-facing interface and story text. Story Mode requires large readable text, strong contrast on warm low-luminance surfaces, keyboard support where applicable, reduced-motion safety, no autoplay, and text available as real HTML rather than baked into images. The product should support parent reading comfort in dim rooms and avoid color-only meaning. Child privacy and age-appropriate design are baseline requirements, not optional compliance extras.
