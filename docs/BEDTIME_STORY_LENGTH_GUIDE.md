# Bedtime Story Length Guide

Status: production standard for GlimmerTales manuscripts.

## Target ranges

- **0-2 years:** 200-500 words, 2-5 minutes.
- **3-5 years:** 500-1,000 words, 5-10 minutes.
- **6-8 years:** 1,000-2,000 words, 10-15 minutes.
- **9-12 years:** 2,000-4,000 words, 15-25 minutes.

## Practical sweet spot

For sleep quality and attention retention, the best default for most children aged 3-8 is an **8-12 minute** story with a clear beginning, middle, and resolution.

The useful rule is: **end the story while the child still wants a little more.**

If the story drags beyond attention span, the child gets restless. If it is too short, it can feel unsatisfying and cause “one more story” negotiation.

## Nightly routine target

1. 2-3 minutes of cuddling or talking.
2. 8-12 minute story.
3. 1 minute of lights-out chat.

For developmental benefit, 10-15 minutes of consistent nightly reading beats occasional longer sessions.

## Product rules

- Avoid highly stimulating cliffhangers, action sequences, and scary content before sleep.
- Use a calming emotional tone.
- End predictably and warmly.
- Keep the final page lower energy than the first page.
- Store target length metadata in story data as `bedtimeLengthGuide`.
- Treat short scaffold text as seed copy until expanded to the production word range.
