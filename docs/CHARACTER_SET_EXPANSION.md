# GlimmerTales Character Set Expansion v0.4

Status: story-seed scaffold. These sets are for quick parent selection and catalog testing. They are not final production character bibles yet.

## Purpose

Parents should be able to choose quickly without reading a whole catalog. Character sets create easy mental shelves:

- familiar friends
- silly friends
- dream friends
- ocean friends
- sleepy lane friends

## Character sets

### Meadow Friends

Existing core cast: Daisy, Dino, Pip, Itsy.  
Use for gentle bravery, nature curiosity, friendship, and bedtime problem-solving.

### Pillow Pals

Stories: `The Pillow Hill Parade`.  
Characters:

- Momo Puffin: round sleepy puffin, peach beak, cloud-soft belly, waddly little leader.
- Bibi Bunny: small lilac bunny, long soft ears, tiny hop, gentle giggle energy.
- Tula Turtle: mint turtle with pillow-like shell, slow and reassuring.

Best moods: Silly, wind-down, cozy body calm.

### Sky Bakers

Stories: `Button Moon Bakery`.  
Characters:

- Coco Cricket: tiny cocoa cricket, round eyes, careful baker energy, not realistic or insect-like.
- Luma Lamb: cream lamb with starry wool tufts, soft helper, soothing presence.
- Nib Starling: small navy bird with sleepy sparkle accents, gentle imagination cue.

Best moods: Dreamy, imagination, quiet wonder.

### Ocean Dreamers

Stories: `Nori and the Bubble Tide`.  
Characters:

- Nori Narwhal: small plush narwhal, mint-blue body, tiny rounded horn, calm breathing anchor.
- Puddle Otter: round brown otter, cozy paws, playful but soft.
- Mimi Minnow: tiny silver-pink fish, simple shape, bubble trail helper.

Best moods: Sleepy, calming, breathing, watery sensory wind-down.

### Lantern Lane

Stories: `Fennel’s Lantern Lane`.  
Characters:

- Fennel Fox: small coral fox, oversized tail, warm guide, non-sneaky.
- Roo Mouse: tiny cream mouse, lantern-seed carrier, careful and brave.
- Opal Owl: soft lavender owl, round glasses-like eye markings without actual glasses, nighttime watcher.

Best moods: Sleepy, home, comfort, safe path.

## Rules for all new sets

- Keep every character original and non-franchise-adjacent.
- Make silhouettes readable at story-card size.
- Avoid realistic insect/spider/predator details.
- Give each set one strong visual palette and one emotional job.
- Do not mix too many sets in one story unless it is a deliberate crossover event.
- Build production master sheets before commissioning full interior art.
